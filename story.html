ONE STORY OF MILLIONS

Millions of children in the Philippines go to school every day without the basic tools to learn. G.I.F.T.Ed is changing that — one pencil at a time.

HIS NAME IS MILJHON

PHOTO COMING SOON

He is 9 years old.
He lives in the Philippines.

His family earns less than $23 a month.
Every morning, he gets up and goes to school anyway.

PHOTO COMING SOON

He borrows his sister's notebook.

He waits for classmates to finish so he can use their pencil.

Some days there isn't one. Some days he just watches.

Without supplies,
kids fall behind.

Fall behind in school.

Fall behind in life.

A pencil costs 10 cents. A notebook costs 50 cents.
These are not luxuries. They are the difference
between a future and no future.

This is why
G.I.F.T.Ed exists.

Founded by two high school juniors from New Jersey, G.I.F.T.Ed raises funds to provide pens, pencils, notebooks and tablets to students in the Philippines who deserve every opportunity to learn.
